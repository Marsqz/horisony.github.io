Blind Date
This is a 2-day project in DSC Design Sprint Workshop in Shanghai. DSC is an association instructing people about Google Design Sprint Design Methods. 6 people including me designed this project which aims to help single young people find the other halves by providing an environment for them to cook together.
Define Problem
My team got a picture on the left, showing a bunch of parents posting their children's information on a blind date square, hoping to find a boyfriend or girlfriend for their children.

We decided to target the young single people, helping them find a mate.
User Research
We went out to do user research, totally 6 people were interviewed. Most of them tend to know the opossite sex from friends or family members, and resent blind date with a total stranger to some extent.

For those who do have a boyfriend or girlfriend, they knew each other from social activities in college.
Brainstorming
After user research and experience map, we went through personal brainstorming and team brainstorming. First, each of us tried to think of as many ideas as possible by our own, one idea on a sticky note. Then we post our ideas on a coordinate axis with Y-axis being "user value" and X-axis being "cost". Then we analyzed our ideas together and voted for them.
Function Design
The highest voted idea is about eating together, so we decided to expand on this idea. We designed some activities for single men and women to get to know each other.
Prototype
Prototype includes 2 parts: online wechat official account and offline cooking activities. For wechat official account, we made paper prototype. For cooking activities, we created the a Blind Date Kitchen with plasticine.
User Testing
4 designers from other design group listened to our idea and gave us suggestions, 2 other people were invited as users to use our product and gave feedback, according to which we improved our design.
